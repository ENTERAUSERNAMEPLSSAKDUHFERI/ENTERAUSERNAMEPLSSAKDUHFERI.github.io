Who Am I

I am a builder and a protector by nature. I'm drawn not to the glamour of cybersecurity, but to its core: a puzzle of cause and effect, where a single detail can be the difference between safety and compromise.

My journey began at 15 with C++. While others sought the right output, I fell in love with the errors. I was captivated by how a missing semicolon could break everything, and the quiet satisfaction of tracing the clues to a fix.

That fascination with finding flaws and understanding systems is my foundation. I moved from theory to practice by building a phishing email to demonstrate how easily trust can be exploited. Seeing credentials appear in my terminal was a pivotal moment.

It was a lesson in human nature and digital safety. It solidified my purpose: to understand these mechanisms not to break things, but to build stronger defences.

I cannot sit idle. There is a bug in me, a bug named Purpose, it guides me, it shows me what to do NEXT.
